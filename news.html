SUNY Binghamton Tournament

Cornell Men's Club Volleyball started off their season at SUNY Binghamton. They brought two teams, Red and Black, to the tournament. Team Red marched through pool play going undefeated and coming out as top seed into tournament play. In the other bracket, Team Black earned second place and a spot in tournament play. The people got what they came for when Team Red and Black squared up in the semi-finals. Team Red took the match in an exciting 2 point victory in game 3. Team Red played a strong men's club team in the finals. Game one went to the Boomers. Game two began with Cornell on a hot streak growing a 10 point lead. Unfortunately, the Boomers rallied back to take game 2 and the match.

Result:
Cornell Black, 3/4th Place
Cornell Red, 2nd Place

University of Rochester Tournament

In the first pool, Cornell faced Rochester Gold in our first match. The good guys were off to a sluggish start, but were lifted by Brad Tiller's outstanding jump serves to claim their first victory of the day. Next on the list was Monroe Community College. Still early in the morning, Cornell slid by with a 2-0 victory led by setter, Matt Herbrand, resulting in a number one seed for redrafting of pools.

For the second pool, Syracuse became the Big Red's next opponent. All around great team play allowed Cornell to take game one. The middles, Matt Thomas and Anthony Point, caught fire in game two hitting 100% each, allowing Cornell to convincingly take game two 25-11. CU faced Canisius College next. Coach Will Pluer made some strategic line up changes, moving middles to the outside and outsides to the middle. This method proved to be effective as Cornell rolled through Canisius 2-0. At the conclusion of pool play, Cornell held the undisputed number one seed for playoffs giving them a first round bye in the 6 team playoff.

In the semi-finals, Pennsylvania College challenged Cornell's unblemished record. With two players over 6'5'', Cornell's defense was not phased. Incredible defense from our liberos/defensive specialists, Reed DesRosiers and Matt Ormseth, proved to be too much for Penn College to handle. Excellent positioning and skill by both players was on full display for this match as Cornell continued their 2-0 win pattern.

Once again, Cornell found themselves in the championship match, this time versus SUNY Brockport. Cornell was well prepared for this challenge. Jacob Sachs held the reigns as setter. Although he was the smallest guy on the court, he had the biggest heart, and that's what counts. His intensity lifted the entire team. Middle, Anthony Point contributed with thunderous blocks on Brockport's go-to player while other middle, Matt Thomas connected on quick after quick with Sachs for a handful of kills. Consistent play all day from Will Valley continued in the finals with timely kills, mostly from the right side with impressive angled swings. Joe Gottwald embraced and thrived in the role as serving specialist, acquiring much needed aces with his smooth jump serve. Only a freshmen, Brad Tiller continues to impress with his mature play, rarely getting blocked and placing the ball in strategic positions. Adam Epstein contributed with his offensive play from the outside as well as back row attacks. None of the offense could have functioned without the continued excellence from Reed DesRosiers and Matt Ormseth on the defensive end. Each of their digs discouraged the opponent and lifted Cornell. In the end, Cornell proved that hard work does pay off, winning the match 2-0, becoming tournament champions.

Result:
Tournament Champions

Home Match Versus Ithaca College

Cornell continues their hot streak after defeating Ithaca College in front of a nearly sold out crowd at Bartels Hall. The atmosphere was electric as the Big Red fought to protect their home court. Senior outside hitter Adam Epstein led the way offensively with 13 kills and 5 service aces. Junior setter Jacob Sachs had a match high 30 assists. Freshman setter Matt Herbrand effectively set, racking up an impressive 24 assists in 2 games. Nine of those assists came from the powerful right hand of sophomore middle Rich Ibsen. Rich also accumulated 4 blocks on the day. Junior outside hitter Will Valley came up big on defense with 8 digs, while freshman outside Brad Tiller contributed 6 digs of his own. Senior Josef Gottwald consistently makes an impact at the service line, earning 3 aces during this match. After this win, Coach Will Pluer maintains his unblemished record.

Result:
19-25, 21-25, 13-25, 22-25, 22-25
5-0 Cornell University
